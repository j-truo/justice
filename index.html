VARIOUS MOVEMENT RESOURCES


hi, i'm jessica truong !! in the past couple weeks, i had the amazing opportunity to participate in a summer program where i was able to learn the basics of creating websites from pure code. i went beyond the program to teach myself more in my free time in order to complete this project. i know i could've easily created a website using much simpler avenues like wix, carrd, etc, but i was really curious to see what i was capable of without that extra help.

in the past week, still a fresh amateur in computer science, i thought, “what better way to practice coding at this time than dedicating my website to current issues?” hours upon hours of commitment and 1,147 lines of code later, i’ve finally accomplished what i had set out to do.

this website is a compilation of resources i've come across fairly recently pertaining to blm, yemen, lgbtq+, indigenous peoples, and so much more. the purpose of this was to provide resources to various issues in one easy-to-navigate area in which people can come back to at their discretion.

yes, the movement "hype" is dying down, but that doesn't mean you should stop educating yourself and taking action. continue to use your privilege for the better but remember to take necessary breaks and heed to your mental and physical health ! i encourage you to explore thoroughly and find yourself more knowledgeable and applicable to these movements. do NOT fall under the illusion that you're an advocate if you refuse to be intersectional. if you have something that could be added to this website, feel free to let me know !! my insta is @j.truo
intersectionality \in-tər-ˌsek-shə-ˈna-lə-tē\ : the complex, cumulative way in which the effects of multiple forms of discrimination (such as racism, sexism, and classism) combine, overlap, or intersect especially in the experiences of marginalized individuals or groups (merriam-webster).
